Activations

Every industry.
One format.

The cube is a compression format for ideas. Any industry that has ideas worth making physical can use it. Here's what that looks like.

Hospitality

Hotel check-in cube

Every guest receives a cube at check-in. The cube contains something from the hotel — a grain of sand from the private beach, a coffee bean from the lobby roast, a thread from the signature linen. The room key is a keycard. The cube is the memory.

The idea inside

Each property chooses what to seal inside. A mountain resort uses a fragment of local stone. A city hotel uses a piece of the skyline — a sliver of glass from a partner building. The cube is a physical sample of the place.

The set mechanic

Hotel chains run a collection across properties. Stay at five locations, collect five cubes. Each one is different. The shelf becomes a travel diary. Loyalty that isn't just points.

Spirits & Wine

The vintage cube

A cube sealed with a drop of the actual liquid inside. The 2024 vintage. The single malt. The small-batch bourbon. You drink the bottle. The cube is the proof it existed.

The idea inside

A single drop of whisky suspended in resin. The color is the vintage. The amber shifts by year. Collect a decade of cubes and you have a color gradient of time.

The activation

Distillery tours end with a cube. Wine club members receive one per shipment. Bars give them to regulars. The cube replaces the coaster, the branded glass, and the loyalty card — all at once.

Automotive

The origin cube

When you take delivery of a car, you receive a cube containing a fragment of the actual material from your vehicle — a chip of the paint color, a sliver of the interior leather, a piece of the carbon fiber trim. The car depreciates. The cube doesn't.

The idea inside

Every cube is specific to the VIN. Your paint color. Your interior spec. The cube is a physical certificate of origin that sits on a shelf, not in a glove box.

The collector effect

Trade up, keep the cube. Three cars over a decade = three cubes. The shelf is your driving history. Manufacturers can also drop limited cubes for concept cars that never shipped.

Music

The album cube

An album release paired with a cube. Not merch. Not a vinyl variant. A physical idea from the record — sealed and permanent. The album is the sound. The cube is the artifact.

Six faces, one record

Each face carries a different element — track listing, credits, artwork, lyrics, studio location, pressing date. The cube is the liner notes made physical. The album art you can hold.

The tour mechanic

Each city on the tour gets a unique cube. Different face design per venue. The tour becomes a collectible map. Fans trade across cities to complete the run.

Fashion

The season cube

Each season, one cube. Six faces for six looks — the collection compressed into a single object. The cube IS the lookbook. Collections come and go. The cube stays.

Six faces, six looks

Each face carries a different element of the collection — a color, a texture, a pattern, a detail. The cube becomes the archive — the part of the collection that doesn't get marked down.

The drop mechanic

Cubes ship with online orders. In-store exclusives get a different edition. Fashion week attendees get a third. Three channels, three cubes, one collection. The cube becomes the reason to show up in person.

Sports

The game cube

A cube containing something from the actual game. Grass from the pitch. Dust from the clay court. Rubber from the track. Water from the pool. You watched the game. The cube was there.

The idea inside

Championship game cubes contain material from the venue that night. The cube is proof the moment happened. Not a ticket stub. Not a photo. A physical sample of the place at the time.

The season mechanic

A cube per home game. Different material each match. Collect the full season. Season ticket holders build a shelf that IS their fandom. Trade with away fans for the complete set.

Real Estate & Architecture

The building cube

A cube containing a material sample from the building itself — concrete from the foundation, glass from the facade, marble from the lobby. Buildings get demolished. The cube is the last fragment.

The idea inside

Developers give cubes to buyers at closing. The cube contains a piece of the building before anyone moved in. The cube is the cornerstone you can hold.

The preservation mechanic

Before a landmark is demolished, produce cubes from its materials. The building lives on as a collectible. Architecture museums, history buffs, former residents — everyone wants a piece.

Museums & Education

The specimen cube

Natural history museums already put things in resin. Now they put ideas in cubes. A moon rock fragment. A fossilized grain. Volcanic ash from a specific eruption. The gift shop becomes a cabinet of curiosities.

The idea inside

Each exhibition gets a cube. The cube contains something related to the show — not a miniature replica, but a material truth. Pigment from a painting's palette. Sand from an archaeological site.

The collection mechanic

Visit five museums in a network, collect five cubes. The shelf becomes a personal museum. Kids start collecting at the natural history museum. Adults continue at the art gallery. Same format. Same shelf.

Technology

The chip cube

A cube containing an actual chip from a previous generation product. The processor that powered your first phone. The GPU that rendered the first frame. Obsolescence, preserved.

The idea inside

Product launches ship with a cube containing a component from the product it replaces. The new replaces the old. The cube memorializes it. A silicon wafer from the last-gen chip. A fragment of Gorilla Glass from the prototype.

The conference mechanic

Tech conferences give cubes instead of lanyards. Each keynote, each track, each workshop — a different cube. The badge is temporary. The cube is permanent. CES, WWDC, I/O — the shelf becomes a career timeline.

Food & Restaurants

The table cube

A cube for the meal. Six faces — one per course. Each face carries the name, the ingredient, the origin. The cube is the menu you take home. The meal is gone. The cube remembers.

Six faces, six courses

Tasting menus become collectible objects. Each face is a course — name, key ingredient, provenance. The cube is the receipt that's worth keeping. Michelin restaurants, pop-ups, chef collaborations.

The restaurant mechanic

Diners collect cubes across restaurants in a city or network. Different chef, different cube, same shelf. The collection becomes a culinary autobiography.

Space & Science

The mission cube

A cube that flew. Payload cubes sent to the ISS, returned to Earth, sealed with a grain of something from the mission. 19mm that left the atmosphere and came back.

The idea inside

A cube containing a fragment of heat shield. A grain of lunar regolith simulant. A piece of the parachute. The cube is the most accessible space artifact ever produced. Not behind glass at the Smithsonian. On your shelf.

The frontier mechanic

Each mission gets a cube. Mars sample return. Artemis landing site. Deep space probes. The shelf becomes a timeline of human exploration. The most important shelf you'll ever build.
