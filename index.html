The Concept

One shape.
Infinite editions.

Cube is a collectible platform built on a single constraint: the 19mm cube. The geometry never changes. The material is the message. A concrete cube and a sterling silver cube say completely different things. Both are unmistakably Cube.

Like a vinyl record is a format — every artist, every genre uses the same 12" disc — Cube is a format for physical collectibles. The constraint is what makes the shelf possible. Brands, artists, events, and franchises all express themselves through the same form. The cube is the brand identity. Everything else is variable.

Cast in clear resin or glass, the cube also has an inside — a vitrine at palm scale. Sand from a specific place, a pressed flower, a chip, a fragment of material. The thing inside gives the cube its meaning. The cube gives the thing inside its permanence.

Why This Shape

The most fundamental
form we have.

Plato associated the cube with the classical element of earth in Timaeus. It is the canonical unit of volume in three-dimensional space — the shape against which all other solids are measured. It appears across toys, games, architecture, crystal structures, and technology. Dice, building blocks, sugar cubes, Rubik's cubes. The form is pre-loaded with cultural meaning across every demographic.

A cube is the simplest three-dimensional object that feels intentional to hold. It sits, stacks, stays. It has weight and presence at a scale between jewelry and sculpture. Every face is a canvas — six distinct surfaces for messages, marks, or edition numbers. And cubes compose: one is an object, two is a pair, six is a set, a shelf of them is a collection. No other collectible form self-organizes like this.

Cubes cast well, mold well, machine well. The form translates across every material without retooling the geometry. Change the material, change the meaning. The form stays. Most collectibles sit behind glass. A cube gets passed around.

IP Integration

Formats are what
other people's IP
plugs into.

Any brand, artist, franchise, or event can express itself through the constraint. A luxury house brings leather. A car brand brings brushed aluminum. A spirits company brings crystal. The material IS the collaboration. Each IP's audience discovers the format. Each format collector discovers new IP. Cross-pollination is built in.

Every collaboration introduces Cube to a new community. The shelf grows. A bigger shelf makes every cube on it more valuable. IP holders aren't making another keychain or t-shirt. They're entering a new category — one with material value, craft integrity, and proven social mechanics. The format is the moat.
