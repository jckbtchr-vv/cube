What We're Building

A collectible format that
any IP can plug into.

Cube is a single, fixed form factor — 19mm — expressed through infinite materials, collaborations, and editions. The geometry never changes. The material is the message.

A vinyl record is a format. Every artist uses the same 12" disc. The constraint is what makes the shelf possible. Cube does this for physical collectibles.

Sterling silver says permanence. Resin says play. Concrete says industrial. Leather says luxury. Same shape. Completely different meaning. And every cube sits on the same shelf.

Partnership Tracks

Three ways in.

Each track is proven by Art Basel. Each one generates a different kind of value.

Track 01

Event Activations

Art fairs, design weeks, conferences, brand summits, festivals
"We turned Art Basel into a game. We can do the same for yours."

Cube designs a custom trading mechanic for your event. Randomized sets force attendees to find each other, transforming passive foot traffic into active participation. You're not adding a vendor. You're adding an experience layer.

Model: Fixed design fee + per-unit production. Event owns the experience. Cube owns the format.
Track 02

Brand Editions

Luxury houses, spirits, automotive, fashion, premium consumer
"Your brand's identity in 19mm of your signature material."

The material IS the collaboration. Leather for a luxury house. Brushed aluminum for a car brand. Crystal for a spirits company. No co-branding gymnastics. The cube speaks your language through what it's made of.

Model: Limited run. Revenue share. Brand brings audience. Cube brings format, production, and provenance.
Track 03

Artist Editions

Contemporary artists, designers, architects, musicians
"One constraint. Every artist arrives at a different answer."

The cube is a brief. 19mm. Six faces. One material. The constraint forces creative decisions that mass merch never requires. Brand editions drive revenue. Artist editions drive culture.

Model: Numbered editions. Revenue share. Sold through both networks.
